title: "Deposits"

A deposit record is created whenever funds arrive at a virtual account (fiat) or deposit address (crypto) connected to an [autoramp flow](autoramp-flows).

## Status

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `RECEIVED` | Funds detected, not yet cleared |
| `CONFIRMED` | Funds cleared — the linked trade will execute |
| `ACTION_REQUIRED` | Manual review needed (e.g. compliance hold) |
| `REJECTED` | Deposit rejected or refunded |

## Fetching Deposits

- [`GET /deposit/list`](api/deposit-list) <Icon icon="link" size={14} /> — paginated, filter by `customerId`, `currency`, or `status`
- [`GET /deposit/detail`](api/deposit-detail) <Icon icon="link" size={14} /> — single deposit by `id`; scoped to the authenticated partner
- [`POST /deposit/submitAttachmentFile`](api/deposit-submit-attachment) <Icon icon="link" size={14} /> — upload a compliance attachment for a deposit stuck in `ACTION_REQUIRED` (max 10MB)

A deposit's `receiverVirtualAccount` or `receiverDepositAddress` field identifies which rail it arrived on; `txid` / `voutIndexTxKey` identify the underlying transaction.

<Note>
  `customerId`, `txFrom`, and `txReference` may be `null` depending on the deposit rail — don't assume they're always populated.
</Note>

## Legacy Deposits

EUR/GBP deposits made under the older deposit flow are tracked as a separate resource with numeric IDs: [`GET /legacy-deposit/list`](api/legacy-deposit-list) <Icon icon="link" size={14} /> and [`GET /legacy-deposit/detail`](api/legacy-deposit-detail) <Icon icon="link" size={14} />. New integrations should not need these — they exist for reconciling historical records. A [Trade](trades) originating from a legacy deposit carries a `legacyDeposit` object instead of `deposit`.

## Monitoring

Subscribe to `DEPOSIT_RECEIVED`, `DEPOSIT_APPROVED`, and `DEPOSIT_REJECTED` rather than polling. See [Webhooks](webhooks) and [Payload Examples](webhooks-payload-examples).

Once a deposit reaches `CONFIRMED`, the linked [Trade](trades) executes automatically under an enabled autoramp flow.
