## Fiat Currencies

The current OpenAPI contract exposes the same fiat `currency` enum — `AUD`, `EUR`, `GBP`, `SGD`, `USD` — across Virtual Account creation, Bank Account creation, and the onramp flow entry (`OnrampFromDto`).

| Currency | Virtual Account (onramp) | Bank Account (offramp payout) | Account Format |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **AUD** | ✅ | ✅ | BSB + Account Number (`payId`, `bicSwift`, `accountNumber`) |
| **EUR** | ✅ | ✅ | IBAN (`iban`, `bicSwift`) |
| **GBP** | ✅ | ✅ | IBAN (`iban`, `bicSwift`) |
| **SGD** | ✅ | ✅ | IBAN (`iban`, `bicSwift`) |
| **USD** | ✅ | ✅ | IBAN (`iban`, `bicSwift`) |

<Note>
  SGD and USD are enabled in the current create schema for both Virtual Accounts and Bank Accounts. Confirm corridor-specific receiving/payout details with Coinut before shipping UX for these currencies — see the [Virtual Accounts Guide](/virtual-accounts-guide) for the full field breakdown.
</Note>

| **USDT** | Crypto Address, Deposit Address, Offramp source, Payment `deductCurrency` |

## Currency Usage by Flow

| Flow | Fiat Side | Crypto Side |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Onramp** | Customer deposits `AUD`/`EUR`/`GBP`/`SGD`/`USD` to a Virtual Account | Coinut settles `USDT`/`USDC` to the customer's crypto address |
| **Offramp** | Coinut pays out `AUD`/`EUR`/`GBP`/`SGD`/`USD` to a customer bank account | Customer sends `USDT`/`USDC` to a Coinut deposit address |
| **Payment** | Payout `currency` to a registered bank account | Balance debited via `deductCurrency` (`USDT` or `USDC`) |

<Note>
  `MockDepositRequestDto` (sandbox-only) accepts all seven currency codes (`EUR`, `GBP`, `SGD`, `USD`, `AUD`, `USDT`, `USDC`) in a single enum for simulating deposits — this is a sandbox testing convenience, not evidence of a unified currency type in production endpoints.
</Note>
